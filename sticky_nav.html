Introduction

Welcome to the sticky nav bar demo! This is a long section meant to provide enough scrollable area to demonstrate how the sticky navigation bar works. Sticky navigation bars are great for enhancing usability on pages with lots of content.

Keep scrolling to see the navigation bar stay at the top as you move down the page. Click the navigation links to smoothly scroll to other sections.

Details

The navigation bar above uses position: sticky and top: 0 to stay visible while you scroll. This approach is modern, reliable in most browsers, and doesn't require JavaScript.

  • HTML Structure: Use a top-level <nav> and section IDs.
  • Anchor Links: <a href="#sectionX"> links scroll smoothly to the correct content.
  • Sticky CSS: position: sticky; top: 0; pins the nav bar to the viewport edge.
  • Smooth Scrolling: scroll-behavior: smooth; on <html> makes jumps elegant.

You can customize section heights, colors, or add more sections as needed.

Conclusion

The sticky nav approach enhances navigation on long pages. It keeps important links always accessible. Try scrolling or clicking the nav links above!

For accessibility, ensure all links use meaningful text. Mouse and keyboard users benefit as well, and screen readers recognize the <nav> element.
